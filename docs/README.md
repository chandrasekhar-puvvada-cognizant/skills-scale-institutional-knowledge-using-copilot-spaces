# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

Welcome to the OctoAcme project management process documentation repository. This folder contains comprehensive guides for all phases of project delivery, from initiation through retrospective and continuous improvement.

## Purpose

This documentation serves as a **single source of truth** for how OctoAcme runs projects. Whether you're onboarding to a new team, leading your first project, or refining our processes, these guides will help you understand our approach, roles, and workflows.

## Core Principles

OctoAcme's project management approach is built on five core principles:

- **Customer-first**: Prioritize customer value and usability in all decisions
- **Iterative delivery**: Deliver small, testable increments to gather feedback early
- **Clear ownership**: Each project has named leaders (PM, Product Lead) with clear responsibilities
- **Data-informed decisions**: Measure impact and iterate based on evidence
- **Psychological safety**: Encourage feedback, learning, and continuous improvement

## Project Lifecycle Overview

Our standard project lifecycle consists of five phases:

1. **Initiation** – Validate business need, align stakeholders, and establish success criteria
2. **Planning** – Break work into shippable increments and create a detailed roadmap
3. **Execution** – Build, test, review, and iterate with regular team rhythm
4. **Release** – Deploy to production and verify functionality with stakeholders
5. **Close & Retrospective** – Capture learnings and feed improvements back into processes

2. Review [OctoAcme Personas](./octoacme-roles-and-personas.md) to understand roles and responsibilities

- Reference the checklists and templates provided in each guide

### For Product Managers
- Focus on [OctoAcme Project Initiation Guide](./octoacme-project-initiation.md) and [Planning](./octoacme-project-planning.md) for defining scope and priorities
- Use the retrospective guide to measure outcomes and inform future roadmap decisions

- Review [OctoAcme Execution & Tracking](./octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md) for workflow and quality standards
- Check [OctoAcme Release & Deployment Guide](./octoacme-release-and-deployment.md) before production deployments
- Participate actively in retrospectives to drive continuous improvement

## Communication Cadence

OctoAcme projects follow a standard communication rhythm:

- **Daily** – Standup meetings with delivery team (15 minutes)
- **Weekly** – PM + Product Manager alignment sync
- **Weekly** – Risk register and blocker review
- **Bi-weekly or Sprint-based** – Demo/review with stakeholders
- **Monthly** – Broader stakeholder updates
- **Post-sprint/release** – Retrospective meeting

## Key Artifacts

Every OctoAcme project should maintain these core documents:

- **Project Charter / One-pager** – Problem statement, goals, success metrics
- **Roadmap and Release Plan** – Timeline, milestones, and dependencies
- **Backlog** – Prioritized list of work with acceptance criteria
- **Risk Register** – Identified risks with mitigation plans
- **Definition of Done** – Criteria for what "done" means for your project
- **Retrospective Notes** – Learnings and action items for continuous improvement
